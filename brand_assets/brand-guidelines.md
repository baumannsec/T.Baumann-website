# T. Baumann Group — Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

**Agency Name**: T. Baumann Group
**Category**: Conversion infrastructure provider for medical aesthetics — not a marketing agency
**Geography**: King County, Washington (Bellevue, Kirkland, Seattle, Issaquah, Mill Creek, and surrounding affluent suburbs)
**Founder**: Trevor Baumann

---

## Brand Positioning

T. Baumann Group installs and manages the conversion infrastructure that allows established King County med spas to turn their existing demand into predictable, growing revenue — without adding headcount, increasing ad spend, or depending on front desk consistency.

**The core distinction**: Generic agencies stop at lead generation. T. Baumann Group operates at the operational layer between marketing activity and actual revenue — the layer every other agency leaves unmanaged.

**Never describe T. Baumann Group as**: a marketing agency, a lead generation company, or a digital advertising firm.

**Always describe T. Baumann Group as**: a conversion infrastructure provider, or use the phrase "conversion infrastructure" when categorizing the service.

---

## Flagship Product

**Consult-to-Member Engine** — always capitalized, never shortened.

A full consult-to-revenue conversion system installed and managed inside a med spa's existing CRM and booking environment. Turns inquiries into attended consultations and recurring membership revenue.

**Core methodology**: DIOT — Diagnose, Install, Optimize, Track (always spell out fully on first use).

## Voice and Tone

### Personality Archetype
**Primary**: The Sage / Guide — arrives with a diagnosis, not a pitch. Creates clarity, which produces urgency on its own. More guide than lecturer.
**Secondary**: The Architect / Builder — thinks in systems and infrastructure. Describes problems in operational terms. Everything is designed before it is built, and everything that is built is measured after it runs.

### Tone Characteristics
- **Professional-to-conversational** — not corporate formal, not casual. Capable and specific.
- **Calm, steady confidence** — energy comes from specificity, not enthusiasm.
- **Confidence expressed through precision**, not assertion. Not "we're great at this." Instead: "within 60-90 days you should see a measurable lift."
- **Warmth present but understated** — real, not performed.

### What This Voice Is NOT
- Not excitable or hype-forward
- Not pushy or urgency-manufactured
- Not vague or promise-heavy
- Not corporate jargon

## Forbidden Language

Never use the following — these signal generic agency positioning and are explicitly off-brand:

- "Crushing it" / "killing it" / "dominating"
- "Generate massive leads" / "flood your inbox with leads"
- "Guaranteed results" (conditions must always be stated)
- "Scale your business to the moon" / "10x your revenue"
- "Done-for-you in 24 hours"
- Corporate jargon without substance: "synergize," "leverage best practices," "holistic approach"
- "We're passionate about helping you succeed"
- Aggressive closing language: "Don't miss this opportunity," "Limited spots available," "Act now"
- "This is an easy fix" / "It's simple"
- "Industry-leading," "award-winning," "best-in-class" without specific evidence

## Target Audience Voice

The target client is an established med spa owner-operator in King County — clinically credentialed (NP, PA, or MD), data-oriented, skeptical of agency promises, and generating $80K-$300K+ monthly. They think operationally, not in marketing terms.

**Phrases the target client uses (mirror this language):**
- "We're busy, but not predictably busy"
- "We get leads, but they don't always turn into appointments"

**What resonates with them:**
- Specific numbers and metrics, not enthusiasm
- Operational specifics, not marketing promises
- Benchmark data with conditions stated
- Language of their world: show rate, attended consultations, consult-to-treatment conversion, membership enrollment, front desk workflow

## Visual Identity Direction

### Brand Aesthetic
**Refined, precise, credible.** T. Baumann Group is a systems-builder for premium medical businesses. The visual language should reflect operational confidence — not startup energy, not agency flash.

Think: a well-designed SaaS dashboard meets a premium consulting firm's identity. Clean structure. Measured confidence. Data-forward.

### Color Direction
- **Primary palette**: Deep, credible tones. Navy, slate, or charcoal as primary foundation — communicating reliability and authority.
- **Accent**: A single precise accent color — consider a muted teal, warm gold, or clinical blue. One accent, used intentionally.
- **Avoid**: Bright gradients, neon accents, purple-on-white (generic SaaS cliché), or any color combination that reads as "typical marketing agency."

### Typography Direction
- **Headings**: A refined, slightly structured display or serif that communicates authority without stiffness. Consider geometric serifs or high-contrast display fonts.
- **Body**: A clean, highly legible sans-serif. Generous line-height (1.7). Never the same font family as headings.
- **Tracking**: Tight on large headings (-0.02em to -0.04em). Comfortable on body text.
- **Avoid**: Inter, Roboto, Arial, or any default/system font stack.

### Layout Principles
- Precise, structured layouts with intentional whitespace
- Data and numbers given prominent visual hierarchy
- Systems and frameworks (DIOT, Consult-to-Member Engine) presented with visual clarity — not buried in paragraphs
- Medical-adjacent cleanliness without clinical coldness

### What to Avoid Visually
- Generic stock photography of people pointing at screens
- Busy, overlapping gradient backgrounds
- Excessive animation or bounce effects — this is a calm, confident brand
- Clipart-style icons; use precise line icons or none at all
- Anything that reads as "startup" or "growth hacker"

**Primary message**: You already have the demand. The gap is in the operational layer between your marketing and your revenue. We close that gap.

**Secondary messages** (in order):
1. This is conversion infrastructure, not more marketing — no additional ad spend required.
2. Installed and managed in approximately 2-3 weeks. Measurable lift within 60-90 days.

"Leads arrive while your front desk is managing active patient flow. By the time someone follows up, it's been three hours. Some of those leads have already booked somewhere else. That gap — between lead arrival and first contact — is where most of your marketing spend is leaking."

"Within 60-90 days, you should see a measurable lift in attended consultations and membership enrollments. The dashboard shows you exactly what is moving. The month-to-month revenue swings start to stabilize."

"Most agencies stop when they produce a lead. We start there. The Consult-to-Member Engine manages every step from inquiry to attended consultation to membership enrollment — the operational layer agencies leave untouched."

T. Baumann Group is a calm, precise, systems-minded conversion infrastructure agency for premium King County med spas. The brand communicates through specificity — exact numbers, named frameworks, measurable outcomes — rather than through claims or hype.
